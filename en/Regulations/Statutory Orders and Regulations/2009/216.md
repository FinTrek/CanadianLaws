# CPFTA Tariff Preference Regulations

**SOR/2009-216**

Enabling authorities: 
- [CUSTOMS TARIFF](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md)

Date registered: 30 July 2009

----------

Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance, pursuant to subsection 16(2)<sup><a href='#fn_1752_hq_7420'>[a]</a></sup> of the [Customs Tariff](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md)<sup><a href='#fn_1752_hq_7421'>[b]</a></sup>, hereby makes the annexed CPFTA Tariff Preference Regulations.

<a name='fn_1752_hq_7420'><sup>a</sup></a>: S.C. 2001, c. 28, s. 34(1)<br />

<a name='fn_1752_hq_7421'><sup>b</sup></a>: S.C. 1997, c. 36<br />




## Interpretation


**1** In these Regulations, ***originating*** means qualifying as originating in the territory of a Party under the rules of origin set out in Chapter Three (Rules of Origin) of the Canada-Peru Free Trade Agreement, signed on May 29, 2008.




## General


**2** For the purposes of paragraph 24(1)(b) of the [Customs Tariff](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md), originating goods exported from Peru are entitled to the benefit of the Peru Tariff if
- **(a)** the goods are shipped to Canada without shipment through another country either
	- **(i)** on a through bill of lading, or
	- **(ii)** without a through bill of lading if the importer provides, when requested by an officer, documentary evidence that indicates the shipping route and all points of shipment and transhipment prior to the importation of the goods; or
- **(b)** the goods are shipped to Canada through another country and the importer provides, when requested by an officer,
	- **(i)** documentary evidence that indicates the shipping route and all points of shipment and transhipment prior to the importation of the goods, and
	- **(ii)** a copy of the customs control documents that establish that the goods remained under customs control while in that other country.
