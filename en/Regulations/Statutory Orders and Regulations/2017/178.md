# Regulations Defining “EU country or other CETA beneficiary”

**SOR/2017-178**

Enabling authorities: 
- [CUSTOMS TARIFF](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md)

Date registered: 1 September 2017

----------

His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance, pursuant to section 49.9<sup><a href='#fn_a'>[a]</a></sup> of the [Customs Tariff](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md)<sup><a href='#fn_b'>[b]</a></sup>, makes the annexed Regulations Defining EU country or other CETA beneficiary.



<a name='fn_a'><sup>a</sup></a>: S.C. 2017, c. 6, s. 97<br />

<a name='fn_b'><sup>b</sup></a>: S.C. 1997, c. 36<br />


## Interpretation


**1** For the purposes of the [Customs Tariff](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md), ***EU country or other CETA beneficiary*** means a country or territory set out in the schedule to these Regulations.




## Coming into Force


<sup><a href='#fn_Ind8411_hq_20657'>[*]</a></sup>**2** These Regulations come into force on the day on which section 97 of the [Canada–European Union Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement Implementation Act](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/2017/c.%206.md), chapter 6 of the Statutes of Canada, 2017, comes into force, but if they are registered after that day, they come into force on the day on which they are registered.

<a name='fn_Ind8411_hq_20657'><sup>*</sup></a>: [Note: Regulations in force September 21, 2017, *see* SI/2017-47.]<br />
