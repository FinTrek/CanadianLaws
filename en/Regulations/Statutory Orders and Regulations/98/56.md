# Refund of Duties on Obsolete or Surplus Goods Regulations

**SOR/98-56**

Enabling authorities: 
- [CUSTOMS TARIFF](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md)

Date registered: 29 December 1997

----------

His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of National Revenue, pursuant to section 112 of the [Customs Tariff](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md)<sup><a href='#fna_e'>[a]</a></sup>, hereby makes the annexed Refund of Duties on Obsolete or Surplus Goods Regulations.

<a name='fna_e'><sup>a</sup></a>: S.C. 1997, c. 36<br />




## Application for a Refund


**1** 

- **(1)** Subject to subsection (2), an application for a refund under section 110 of the [Customs Tariff](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md) must be accompanied by
	- **(a)** a document certified by an officer that identifies the obsolete or surplus goods; and
	- **(b)** a list of the documents required under Part I of the [Accounting for Imported Goods and Payment of Duties Regulations](/en/Regulations/Statutory%20Orders%20and%20Regulations/86/1062.md) that relate to the importation of those goods.

- **(2)** Where an officer is not available to certify the document referred to in paragraph (1)(a), other non-certified documentation that contains similar information may be substituted for it.




## Coming into Force


**2** These Regulations come into force on January 1, 1998.
