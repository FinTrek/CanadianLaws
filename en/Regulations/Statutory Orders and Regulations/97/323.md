# CCFTA Rules of Origin for Casual Goods Regulations

**SOR/97-323**

Enabling authorities: 
- [CUSTOMS TARIFF](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md)

Date registered: 5 July 1997

----------

His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance, pursuant to subsection 13(2)<sup><a href='#fn_SOR-97-323_e_hq_5747'>[a]</a></sup> of the [Customs Tariff](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md)<sup><a href='#fn_SOR-97-323_e_hq_5748'>[b]</a></sup>, hereby makes the annexed CCFTA Rules of Origin for Casual Goods Regulations.

<a name='fn_SOR-97-323_e_hq_5747'><sup>a</sup></a>: S.C. 1994, c. 47, s. 75(1)<br />

<a name='fn_SOR-97-323_e_hq_5748'><sup>b</sup></a>: R.S., c. 41 (3rd Supp.)<br />




## Interpretation


**1** In these Regulations, ***casual goods*** means goods other than goods imported for sale or for an industrial, occupational, commercial or institutional or other like use.




## Casual Goods


**2** Casual goods that are acquired in Chile are deemed to originate in Chile and are entitled to the benefit of the Chile Tariff if
- **(a)** the marking of the goods is in accordance with the marking laws of Chile and indicates that the goods are the product of Chile or Canada; or
- **(b)** the goods do not bear a mark and there is no evidence to indicate that the goods are not the product of Chile or Canada.




## Coming into Force


<sup><a href='#fn_SOR-97-323_e_hq_5749'>[*]</a></sup>**3** These Regulations come into force on the day on which section 9 of the [Canada-Chile Free Trade Agreement Implementation Act](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2014.md), chapter 14 of the Statutes of Canada, 1997, comes into force.
