# Certain Ships Remission Order, 2010

**SOR/2010-203**

Enabling authorities: 
- [CUSTOMS TARIFF](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md)

Date registered: 23 September 2010

----------

Her Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance, pursuant to section 115<sup><a href='#fn_81000-2-704E_hq_9721'>[a]</a></sup> of the [Customs Tariff](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md)<sup><a href='#fn_cargo_e_hq_9268'>[b]</a></sup>, hereby makes the annexed Certain Ships Remission Order, 2010.

<a name='fn_81000-2-704E_hq_9721'><sup>a</sup></a>: S.C. 2005, c. 38, par. 145(2)(j)<br />

<a name='fn_cargo_e_hq_9268'><sup>b</sup></a>: S.C. 1997, c. 36<br />




## Remission


**1** Remission of customs duties paid under the [Customs Tariff](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md) is granted to the following:
- **(a)** Algoma Central Corporation, St. Catharines, Ontario, in respect of the tankers *Algonova* and *AlgoCanada;* and
- **(b)** British Columbia Ferry Services Inc., Victoria, British Columbia, in respect of the vessels *Coastal Renaissance*, *Coastal Inspiration*, *Coastal Celebration* and *Northern Expedition*.




## Conditions


**2** The remission is granted on the following conditions:
- **(a)** the importer files all evidence that is required by the Canada Border Services Agency to determine eligibility for remission; and
- **(b)** a claim for remission is made by the importer to the Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness within two years after the day on which this Order comes into force.




## Coming into Force


**3** This Order comes into force on the day on which it is registered.
