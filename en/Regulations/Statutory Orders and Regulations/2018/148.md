## Blood Alcohol Concentration and Blood Drug Concentration



**Summary offence**

**1** For the purpose of paragraph 253(3)(b) of the [Criminal Code](/en/Acts/Revised%20Statutes%20of%20Canada/C/C-46.md), the prescribed blood drug concentration for tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) is 2 ng of THC per mL of blood.

**2** For the purpose of paragraph 253(3)(a) of the [Criminal Code](/en/Acts/Revised%20Statutes%20of%20Canada/C/C-46.md), the prescribed blood drug concentration for each drug set out in column 1 of the table to this section is set out in column 2.

**Hybrid offence — combination of drugs and alcohol**

**3** For the purpose of paragraph 253(3)(c) of the [Criminal Code](/en/Acts/Revised%20Statutes%20of%20Canada/C/C-46.md), the prescribed blood alcohol concentration is 50 mg of alcohol per 100 mL of blood and the prescribed blood drug concentration for tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) is 2.5 ng of THC per mL of blood.
