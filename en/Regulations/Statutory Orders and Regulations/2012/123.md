# “SeaRose FPSO” Repair or Alteration Remission Order, 2012

**SOR/2012-123**

Enabling authorities: 
- [CUSTOMS TARIFF](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md)

Date registered: 8 June 2012

----------

His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Minister of Finance, pursuant to section 115<sup><a href='#fn_a'>[a]</a></sup> of the [Customs Tariff](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md)<sup><a href='#fn_b'>[b]</a></sup>, makes the annexed “SeaRose FPSO” Repair or Alteration Remission Order, 2012.



<a name='fn_a'><sup>a</sup></a>: S.C, 2005, c. 38, par. 145(2)(j)<br />

<a name='fn_b'><sup>b</sup></a>: S.C. 1997, c. 36<br />


## Remission


**1** Remission is granted to Husky Oil Operations Ltd., Calgary, Alberta, of the customs duties paid or payable under the [Customs Tariff](/en/Acts/Statutes%20of%20Canada/1997/c.%2036.md) in respect of the “SeaRose FPSO” that was returned to Canada after having been exported in 2012 for repair or alteration.




## Conditions


**2** The remission is granted on the following conditions:
- **(a)** the importer files, as requested, all evidence that is required by the Canada Border Services Agency to determine eligibility for remission; and
- **(b)** a claim for remission is made by the importer to the Minister of Public Safety and Emergency Preparedness within two years after the day on which this Order comes into force.




## Coming into Force


**3** This Order comes into force on the day on which it is registered.
