# Order Transferring to the Minister of National Revenue, the Powers, Duties and Functions of the Minister of Industry under the Tax Rebate Discounting Act

**SI/95-102**

Enabling authorities: 
- [PUBLIC SERVICE REARRANGEMENT AND TRANSFER OF DUTIES ACT](/en/Acts/Revised%20Statutes%20of%20Canada/P/P-34.md)

Date registered: 20 September 1995

----------

Whereas the expression “Minister of Consumer and Corporate Affairs” in the definition ***Minister*** in subsection 2(1) of the [Tax Rebate Discounting Act](/en/Acts/Revised%20Statutes%20of%20Canada/T/T-3.md) was replaced by the expression “Minister of Industry” pursuant to paragraph 62(1)(t) of the Department of Industry Act<sup><a href='#fn_SI-95-102_e_hq_6600'>[*]</a></sup>, which Act was brought into force by Order in Council P.C. 1995-536 of March 29, 1995<sup><a href='#fn_SI-95-102_e_hq_6601'>[**]</a></sup>;

<a name='fn_SI-95-102_e_hq_6600'><sup>*</sup></a>: S.C. 1995, c. 1<br />

<a name='fn_SI-95-102_e_hq_6601'><sup>**</sup></a>: SI/95-48, 1995 Canada Gazette Part II, p. 1013<br />

Therefore, His Excellency the Governor General in Council, on the recommendation of the Prime Minister, pursuant to paragraph 2(a) of the [Public Service Rearrangement and Transfer of Duties Act](/en/Acts/Revised%20Statutes%20of%20Canada/P/P-34.md), is pleased hereby to transfer from the Minister of Industry to the Minister of National Revenue the powers, duties and functions of the Minister of Industry under the [Tax Rebate Discounting Act](/en/Acts/Revised%20Statutes%20of%20Canada/T/T-3.md).
